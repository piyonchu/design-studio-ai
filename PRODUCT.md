# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CanonForge serves visual creators and small creative teams building large sets of consistent raster assets. The first deep workflow is for 2D game creators who need sprites, props, tiles, UI assets, and audio loops that can move from generation into review, reuse, and engine export. Adjacent users include manhwa/webtoon artists, illustrators, and marketing teams who need coherent visual variants inside a shared project memory.

Users are usually in a production workflow, not a browsing mood: they are seeding a reference, deriving variants, reviewing candidates, organizing approved work, checking lineage/canon drift, and exporting packs for downstream tools.

## Product Purpose

CanonForge helps teams turn one approved reference into a consistent asset library. It binds generation and derivation to a versioned project canon, records provenance, gates quality through review, feeds approved exemplars back into future generation, and exports organized packs for tools like Godot and Unity.

Success means the interface makes consistency, reuse, review, and export feel like one continuous studio loop instead of a set of isolated AI prompts.

## Brand Personality

Expert, focused, precise. The product should feel like a serious creative tool that remembers a team's art direction, exposes provenance clearly, and reduces repetitive production work without flattening the creator's judgment.

The voice should be concise and concrete: describe what will happen, what changed, what is stale, what is ready to export, and what still needs review.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a text-to-image slot machine, a generic asset manager, or a novelty AI playground. Avoid interfaces that celebrate raw generation while hiding consistency, lineage, and review.

Do not drift back into Figma/v0-style page layout or UI-code generation. CanonForge is for visual/raster assets and exportable creative libraries, not website layout, app components, or structured design DSLs.

Avoid generic SaaS marketing tropes in product surfaces: ornamental metrics, decorative gradients, vague "magic" copy, and UI that looks impressive while obscuring the user's next production step.

## Design Principles

Make the canon visible. Every generation, derivation, review decision, and stale asset should make its relationship to the project canon understandable.

Preserve creative control. AI output starts as a candidate; creator approval, rejection, exemplar selection, and export decisions remain explicit.

Prioritize workflow continuity. The path from reference to variant to review to collection to export should stay close at hand without forcing users through disconnected screens.

Show provenance before polish. Lineage, status, source kind, canon version, and export readiness are product information, not metadata to hide.

Respect production density. The interface can be compact and information-rich as long as actions, states, and feedback remain legible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for product surfaces. Body text and controls should maintain AA contrast, focus states must be visible, and important status information should not rely on color alone.

Support reduced motion and avoid motion that blocks task flow. Animation should communicate state changes, loading, selection, and feedback without delaying the user.
